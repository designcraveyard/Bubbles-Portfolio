How I Learn

My Design Process

I'm a self-taught designer using the same principles I used to teach school students — curiosity, structure, and repetition. Claude is my design mentor.

01

Pick a challenge

Each day starts with a fresh design prompt. Claude asks thought-provoking questions about my real-life observations to set the context.

02

Study real apps

I explore competitor apps in the wild — using them, noting what works, what doesn't, and building my own perspective before designing.

03

Discuss & distill

I log my observations and discuss them with Claude. Together we arrive at fundamental UX principles for that specific screen.

04

Design in Figma

With a clear brief, I design in Figma using auto layout and iOS/Android component libraries. Share, get feedback, iterate, repeat.

05

Review & iterate

I share each version with Claude for critique. Each round sharpens the design and deepens my understanding of why things work.

06

Prototype & reflect

Once the design feels right, I build an interactive prototype — then step back to absorb what I learned that day.

I've been doing this daily for the last 3 months. I take pride in being self-taught — and I'm looking for real-world projects to collaborate on and keep learning.
